About Kitcher

Born from the student experience

I am Kitcher Gabriel Tetteh — a student of the Faculty of Biochemistry and Biotechnology at KNUST. I am not running because I have always been in a position of power. I am running because I know what it feels like to be an ordinary student with extraordinary needs that go unheard.

My campaign is rooted in one fundamental belief: that every student at KNUST deserves leadership that genuinely sees them — across every faculty, every hall, every background.

My Vision

What I stand for

🎓

Academic Excellence

Strengthening academic welfare systems, improving library access, and building transparent pathways for student concerns to reach decision-makers.

💡

Student Innovation

Creating pipelines between KNUST students and industry — internships, mentorship programs, and entrepreneurship support for every faculty.

🤝

Inclusive Representation

Ensuring every college, hall, and student group has a genuine seat at the table — not just during campaigns, but every single day.

🏥

Student Welfare

Improving access to health services, mental wellness support, and emergency student funds that actually reach those who need them.

📶

Digital Access

Advocating for reliable campus internet, accessible digital learning tools, and tech infrastructure that matches KNUST's world-class ambitions.

🔒

Campus Safety

Improving lighting, security infrastructure, and accountability systems to make KNUST a place every student feels safe — day and night.

The Platform

Specific. Achievable. Yours.

01

The Student Voice Initiative

A structured, regular system for collecting and acting on student feedback — not suggestion boxes that gather dust, but a real feedback loop between the SRC and the student body with published responses and timelines.

Governance
02

KNUST Career Bridge Program

A faculty-specific internship and mentorship pipeline connecting students with industry professionals and alumni — making the transition from campus to career smoother for every discipline.

Career Development
03

Wellness & Mental Health Fund

Dedicated resources and peer support structures for student mental health, in partnership with the KNUST Hospital and qualified professionals — because academic success starts with personal wellbeing.

Student Welfare
04

Campus Digital Upgrade Advocacy

A sustained push to improve WiFi access, digital learning platforms, and computer lab availability — working directly with KNUST management to deliver measurable improvements within the academic year.

Technology
05

Emergency Student Support Fund

A transparent, accessible emergency financial support system for students facing unexpected hardships — with clear criteria, fast processing, and real accountability on how funds are managed.

Financial Welfare
